Calculus in Animal Husbandry

Optimizing Broiler Growth Rates in Malaysian Poultry Systems

🌱 The Bioscience Scenario

In Malaysian commercial broiler production (e.g., Cobb 500), understanding the instantaneous growth rate is vital for optimizing Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR). If we only look at weight, we miss the moment when efficiency peaks.

By applying Derivatives to the weight function $f(t)$, we determine exactly how many grams of body mass the bird is gaining at any specific point in its 42-day cycle. This allows husbandry experts to adjust protein intake right before the growth rate plateaus.

Data Source Reference: Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute (MARDI) - "Broiler Growth Performance in Tropical Environments" & DVS Malaysia Poultry Statistics.

The Mathematics

The Weight Function (Logistic)
f(x) = L / (1 + e-k(x-x₀))

Where L = Max weight (2800g), k = growth constant, x₀ = peak growth day (day 28).

The Growth Rate (Derivative)
f'(x) = (L · k · e-k(x-x₀)) / (1 + e-k(x-x₀))²

f'(x) represents the weight gain in grams per day at time x.
